It's Not All Mai Tai's

19 June 2013

Yesterday on my Facebook I posted this link: Why You Should Quit Your Job and Travel Now. This is a great article about the joy of world travel and why it’s never going to be a good time, so you should make it time now. I’m ready to travel. I’ve been working remotely for a little over 6 months, unfortunately I have only been working remotely part-time and I have still had the luxury of a day job. While I can’t wait to make remote work my everyday and not just my late at night and my weekend, I can say without a doubt it won’t be that dream that people seem to have of it. I won’t be clicking away on my keyboard at a beach in Hawaii - not everyday at least. No — remote work will put me closer to my family. I will spend an hour on the floor of my apartment playing with my 9-month-old while struggling with a solution to a problem. I will hear her laugh while I’m on the couch debugging. I will go for an afternoon walk with my wife and daughter right in the middle of the so called work day. I know my most valuable coding is done in the few hours in the morning when my family is still sleeping and the couple of hours I stay up after they’ve gone to bed. Those are the times I will code — not the 8 hour chunk in the middle of the day. Hours are bullshhh, cubicles are bullshhh, and life is good.
